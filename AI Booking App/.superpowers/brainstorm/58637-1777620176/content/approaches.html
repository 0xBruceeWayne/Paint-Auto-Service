3 Structural Approaches

All use scroll-based 3D background, Liquid Glass UI, and the AI wizard demo. The difference is how sections are arranged and how the 3D atmosphere shifts.

A

Immersive Single Scroll ⭐ Recommended

One long page. Hero = full-screen AI wizard live demo over a 3D cloudscape. Scroll down → 3D scene morphs into destination cards, feature reveals, testimonials, pricing, footer. The wizard reappears as a floating sticky bubble. Each section gets its own atmosphere (clouds → ocean → city skyline → starfield). Clean, one URL, zero friction.

Pros

  • Maximises 3D scroll impact
  • User never leaves the experience
  • Single file, simple to build

Cons

  • Long page needs tight pacing
B

Landing + App Split

Two routes: / is pure cinematic marketing (no wizard), /app launches the full AI wizard flow as a separate full-screen app. Landing CTA = "Start Planning →" opens /app. Clean brand/product separation.

Pros

  • Landing page uncluttered
  • App can evolve independently

Cons

  • Two pages to build and maintain
  • Breaks immersion on CTA click
C

Wizard-Forward with Scrollable Backstory

Page opens with the AI wizard fullscreen (no navbar, no hero text). After booking a "demo trip," the page slides down revealing marketing sections — features, destinations, testimonials. Inverts the traditional flow: experience first, pitch second.

Pros

  • Most original / memorable
  • AI demo is unavoidable

Cons

  • Unconventional — risky if users bounce before scrolling
